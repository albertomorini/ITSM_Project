\subsection{SLA Compliance Rate}

SLA Compliance Rate can provide a measure whether contractual service levels are respected, thus to accomplish the agreements made with customers or stakeholders and therefore maintaint the quality of the service provided.


For this KPI different process are affected, the first is the ``Service Level Management'', ``Incident Management'', Request 

For this KPI the process affected are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Service Level Management}: to ensure an opportune level of service provided that should ensure what agreed in SLA
    \item \textbf{Incident Management}: to ensure that eventual incident is correctly resolved within time and quality specified.
    \item \textbf{Request Fulfillment}: to fulfill customer and user requests according the agreed specifications
    \item \textbf{Availabiliy Management}: to ensure the service is available for the user and the downtime dued to incidents or maintenance do not take more than necessary resources (time and costs) 
    \item \textbf{Capacity Management}: to monitor that the service fulfill the capacity and performance designed.
    \item \textbf{Supplier Management}: whenever a process involves suppliers and third part activities not strictly dependent by the organization.
\end{itemize}

To measure how many incident occur dued to problems that are not already been resolved.\\
ITSM process involved are

\begin{itemize}

\subsection{Rework rate}


For instance, since the Bank aims to increase revenue from its Internet Banking services, poor user experience caused by application malfunctions or insufficient service quality may reduce customer engagement. At the same time, these defects can represent significant rework for both external suppliers and the internal design and development teams; increasing costs and delaying service improvements, reducing the overall efficiency of the Service Value Chain.

The rate of the rework activities serve a strong KPI for user engagement and involves many process of IT Service Management, main of them are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Change Enablement}: since every change request must be valuated and analyzed.
    \item \textbf{Release Management}: a rework require a further deployment of the new version of the service.
    \item \textbf{Service Validation and Testing}: in order to check that the service meets the desired customer requests.
\end{itemize}
